Biography

I'm a PhD candidate in Genomic Signal Processing (GSP) lab in Electrical and Computer Engineering department of Texas A&M University working under supervision of professor Xiaoning Qian. My research interests are Bayesian Machine Learning and its application in analyzing heterogeneous and high dimensional biological data with a focus on transcriptomics.

A significant part of my research is developing tools to analyze Single-cell RNA sequencing data more efficiently in terms of both accuracy and time complexity. With this goal in mind, I have developed SimCD a unified Bayesian method to simultaneously perform cell clustering and gene differential expression analysis. SimCD is also capable of including both gene-and cell-level biological explanatory variables to better model scRNA-seq data. To learn more about the the model, you can check our preprint paper. As of having interest in solving challenges in scRNA-seq data analysis, I participated in IEEE COVID-19 Single-cell transcriptomics Data Hackathon where I won first place by presenting Biomarker identification for COVID-19 severity based on BALF scRNA-seq data. To learn more about this work you can check on this Github page.

Apart from academic research, I have experience working in collaborative environments right alongside engineers, scientists and biologists. During summer of 2021 I did an internship as a Bioinformatics scientist with Department of Quantitative health sciences at Mayo Clinic. My internship project was mainly focused on gene-drug relationship discovery and learning interactions between genes, drugs and diseases.
